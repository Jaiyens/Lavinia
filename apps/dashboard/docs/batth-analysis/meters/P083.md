## (b) RATE SCHEDULE -- decoded

**Stored schedule: `AGC` -- AG-C Schedule AG, 35+ kW high-use (current default).**

- **Current** Schedule AG, high-use sub-rate for installations **>=35 kW**. AG-C is the **demand-heavy** rate: it trades big fixed/demand cost for the cheapest energy.
- **Charge structure:** highest customer charge of the AG family (**$1.43343/meter/day, ~$43.58/mo**), a **Max Demand charge ($21.43/kW secondary)**, a **Summer Peak Demand charge ($29.92/kW)** on the highest 15-min demand in the **5-8pm** window (summer = Jun-Sep), the lowest AG energy ($0.21329/kWh peak-summer), and the AG-C-only **Demand Charge Rate Limiter** ($0.50/kWh blended cap).
- **The arbitrage:** AG-C only wins when the meter pushes serious kW for many hours (high load factor). PG&E defaults a meter to AG-C the moment metered demand touches 35 kW, but never demotes it back automatically. A low-load-factor pump stranded on AG-C pays ~$43.58/mo + demand charges it no longer justifies, and would pay only ~$0.69/day with **no demand charge on AG-A1**.
