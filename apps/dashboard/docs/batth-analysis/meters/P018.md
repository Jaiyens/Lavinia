## (b) RATE SCHEDULE decoded in plain English — AG-5B (legacy large TOU agricultural)

**What it is.** AG-5B is a **closed legacy** large time-of-use agricultural schedule (Schedule AG-5, rate B/E). It has been closed to new enrollment since March 1, 2020; surviving customers are kept on it only because they are behind-the-meter **solar-legacy-TOU** customers (held until 10 years after permission-to-operate, no later than **July 31, 2027**). AG-5 itself is scheduled to expire in 2027.

**Its charge structure (total bundled, eff. 3/1/2026, from the rate brief):**
- **Customer charge: $1.19446 / meter / day** (≈ $36.31/mo at 30.4 days). This is the line that actually shows up on P018's bill.
- **Max-Demand charge: $27.89/kW summer, $20.54/kW winter** (only billed when the pump runs and sets a demand).
- **Max PEAK-Demand charge: $10.45/kW summer**, assessed on the highest 15-min demand inside the legacy **noon–6 p.m.** peak window.
- **Season:** Summer = May–Oct, Winter = Nov–Apr (a *wider* summer than the modern Schedule AG's Jun–Sep).
- **TOU windows (single-peak B rate):** Summer Peak **12 p.m.–6 p.m. Mon–Fri**; Summer Off-Peak all other hours; Winter Part-Peak 8:30 a.m.–9:30 p.m. Mon–Fri; Winter Off-Peak otherwise.
- **Generation energy** is cheap (AG-5B summer ≈ $0.085/kWh gen-only); AG-5's defining feature is *heavy demand charges with cheap energy.*

**WHY this meter is on AG-5B (and why that is correct, not a mistake).** P018 is **NEM2-paired** (solarFlag true, group 4433, NEM2AA). The whole point of holding these solar pumps on a closed legacy TOU schedule is to keep the **noon–6 p.m. peak window**, which overlaps the midday solar production curve so that solar offsets the most expensive (peak) hours. The modern Schedule AG moved peak to **5–8 p.m.**, which no longer overlaps midday solar. So the legacy rate is *intentional* — moving P018 to Schedule AG early would **destroy** its solar value. This is exactly the rate-arbitrage engine's "hold until 2027" finding (see section e, Finding 3). At the forced 2027 transition, AG-5B → AG-C, and the AG-A1 demotion analysis should be re-run *only then* if the meter is idle / confirmed < 35 kW.
