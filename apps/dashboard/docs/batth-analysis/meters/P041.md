## (b) RATE SCHEDULE decoded in plain English

**Stored / billed schedule: AG-C** (bill prints it as **"AGC Ag35+ kW High Use"**). The lever maps `AGC → card plan AG-C2` (the large, 35-kW-and-up row of the AG-C family).

What AG-C is, in operator English:
- It is the **agricultural large-pump TOU (time-of-use) schedule** for services whose demand reaches **35 kW or more** ("Ag35+"). PG&E puts bigger pumps here. A 1,300 GPM pump comfortably belongs in the 35+ class when it actually runs.
- **Customer (meter) charge:** about **$43.60/month**, prorated to **$1.43343/day**. That daily rate is exactly what this bill printed, so the AG-C mapping is confirmed against the real bill, not guessed.
- **Demand charge ($/kW):** AG-C carries a **maximum-demand charge of $24.95 per kW** (winter and summer), PLUS a **summer peak-window demand charge of $9.00 per kW** (the 5–8pm "peak-period" demand component, applied May–Oct only). The maximum-demand charge is what billed this cycle.
- **Energy ($/kWh), TOU windows:** AG-C is the *low-energy / high-fixed* trade: cheap kWh, expensive fixed/demand. Summer energy ≈ $0.22 peak / $0.20 partial-peak / $0.18 off-peak; winter ≈ $0.1843 / $0.17 / $0.15861. The **peak window is 5–8pm year-round**; partial-peak shoulders it; everything else is off-peak. PG&E agricultural summer is **May–October**.
- **Why this meter is on AG-C:** a large, intermittently-run irrigation pump that draws a high peak kW but few total kWh is *cheaper* on AG-C's low energy rates than on the small-pump AG-A schedules — provided it actually runs enough hours for the cheap energy to repay the $43.60 customer charge and the $24.95/kW demand. AG-C is the right *family* for a 1,300 GPM pump; whether the *exact tier* is optimal can only be judged with real run-hour interval data (see section e).

## (e) EVERY applicable savings finding FOR THIS METER (full arithmetic + engine + data have/need)

All dollar figures below are produced by **deterministic pure functions in `src/lib/energy`** — no AI is involved in any savings number. (The only AI in the whole pipeline is the bill-PDF *vision extraction* in `src/lib/extract`, which read these numbers off the PDF; it does not compute savings.)

### 1. Rate optimization — `rateLever(input, card)` in `src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`

- **What it does:** maps the printed schedule (`AGC` → card plan `AG-C2`, large tier), back-tests the meter's own billed cycles against the dated rate card (must land within the 5% `BACK_TEST_BAND_PCT`), then prices the *same billed usage* on every eligible current candidate (`CANDIDATE_SCHEDULES.large = ["AG-B2","AG-C2"]`) and quotes the cheapest, fail-closed.
- **Result for P041: NO defensible savings figure.** Reasons, deterministically:
  1. **Only one cycle on file, and it is winter and idle (0 kWh).** The lever's `no_usage_basis` guard (`!plan.legacy && totalKwhTested <= 0`) returns `{ kind: "none", reason: "no_usage_basis" }` for a current-schedule meter with zero billed kWh. AG-C2 is a *current* (non-legacy) plan, so the guard fires: **you cannot justify switching a usage-tiered current schedule off a single idle winter bill** — summer run-hours would flip the math.
  2. Even setting that aside, with 0 kWh the only modeled difference between AG-B2 and AG-C2 is the customer charge ($27.85/mo AG-B2 vs $43.60/mo AG-C2) and the demand rate ($13.95/kW vs $24.95/kW). On the printed 0.16 kW that is a *winter-idle* artifact, not a real operating cost — exactly the "sign-ambiguous, intervals-needed" case the ground truth warns about. The engine **correctly emits nothing.**
- **Data we HAVE:** one reconciled winter cycle, the printed schedule, GPM 1,300, status GOOD.
- **Data we NEED to ever quote a rate-switch dollar:** **15-minute interval kWh across a summer run season** (May–Oct), so the lever can price AG-C2 vs AG-B2 vs AG-A2 on real peak/partial/off-peak energy and a real summer peak-window demand kW. Bill summaries carry no kWh, so no trustworthy figure is possible from this data alone. **There is no >$500/yr rate finding here** — and it would be dishonest to invent one from an idle bill.

### 2. Bill audit — `billAudit(input)` in `src/lib/energy/bill-audit.ts`

- **What it does:** compares each posted cycle's total to the **median of the meter's OTHER same-season cycles** (deterministic median, no re-pricing, no AI). Flags only when dollars jump but usage/peak does not. Needs `minComparators` (default **3**) same-season comparable cycles.
- **Result for P041: nothing flags.** Only **1 cycle** is on file (`cycleCount = 1`), far below the 3-comparator floor. The audit cannot run. No finding.
- **Data we NEED:** ≥3 same-season cycles of history for this meter (12+ months of bills) to establish a stable median.

### 3. Solar / NEM checks — `solarNemChecks(input)` + `nemDemandInsight(input)` in `src/lib/energy/solar-nem.ts`

- `solarNemChecks` emits (a) a "watch" note that solar may not cover the evening demand-charge peak, and (b) an **info-severity** December true-up tracking reminder (because `nemType` and `trueUpMonth` are both set). These are *legibility* notes, **not dollar savings** — neither carries `impactUsd`.
- `nemDemandInsight` would quantify the offsettable demand share, but it needs the summer peak-window demand kW, which a winter idle bill does not carry → no dollar figure.
- The printed true-up is **$2.90/year** — immaterial. **No >$500/yr NEM finding.**

### 4. Demand / efficiency

- Billed max demand this cycle = 0.16 kW (standby). With 0 run-kWh there is no kW-per-gallon efficiency signal to compute. `classify.ts` would tag this cycle's signature as effectively idle. No finding.

**Net for P041: zero defensible savings findings.** Every engine fails closed for the honest reason that the only data on file is a single idle winter bill. The right product output is *legibility*, not a dollar.
