## (b) RATE SCHEDULE decoded in plain English

**Stored schedule: `AG5C` — billed as "AG5C Large Time-of-Use Agricultural Power" (legacy AG-5C).**

- **What it is:** AG-5 is a **legacy (closed) PG&E agricultural time-of-use schedule.** New meters cannot be put on it anymore; existing meters are grandfathered. The "C" is the size/tier letter. In the energy engine `familyOf()` folds `AG-5C` to the **`AG-5` family** and marks it `legacy: true` — meaning AG-5 is a valid *source* schedule the rate engine will try to move a meter OFF of, but never a *target* it would move a meter ONTO.
- **Customer charge:** This meter's bill shows a **daily customer (service) charge of $5.30871/day** (a flat fixed charge you pay whether the pump runs or not — that is why an idle pump still produced a $159.64 bill).
- **Demand $/kW:** AG-5C is a **demand-carrying TOU schedule.** The latest bill shows a Max Demand charge (~$15.56/kW pre-03/01, $14.90/kW post-03/01) plus a Max Part-Peak demand adder (~$3.67/kW then $3.51/kW). On an idle meter these resolve to pennies because billed demand was only **0.02 kW**.
- **TOU windows:** AG-5 is a time-of-use schedule with peak / part-peak / off-peak energy windows. The demand-carrying peak window the engine prices against is the **5–8pm rate peak** (`tou.ts`). The latest winter cycle carried **no TOU energy line items at all** (0 kWh in every bucket), so no peak/off-peak energy charges appear.
- **WHY it is on this rate:** It is on AG-5C because it is a **legacy meter that was never migrated** to the current AG-A/AG-B/AG-C families. This is exactly the "183 meters on mixed legacy/current rates almost guarantees some are wrong" situation. Because AG-5 is closed, the rate lever's job is to test whether the **same billed usage would be cheaper on a current eligible schedule** (AG-A1/AG-A2 for a small meter; AG-B2/AG-C2 for a large meter).
