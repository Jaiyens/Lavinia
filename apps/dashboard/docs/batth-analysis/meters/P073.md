## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded

- **Stored schedule:** `AGC` → **AGC / AG-C (modern Schedule AG, ≥35 kW, high use) — secondary voltage.**
- **Plain English:** `AGC` is the **modern (current) Schedule AG, Rate AG-C** — the ≥35 kW *high-use* default. AG-C is the **demand-heavy** AG sub-rate: a **Max-Demand charge ($21.43/kW secondary)** *plus* a punishing **Summer Peak-Demand charge of $29.92/kW** on the highest 15-min demand during the **5-8pm** window in summer, plus the highest AG customer charge (~$43.58/mo). In exchange the *energy* is the cheapest of the four (peak-summer ~$0.213/kWh).
- **Demand Charge Rate Limiter:** AG-C is the only AG sub-rate with the $0.50/kWh blended cap (ex-customer-charge), which protects spiky low-energy meters.
- **The core arbitrage:** AG-C only pays off when the meter pushes serious sustained kW. A meter whose *real* max 15-min demand now sits **comfortably below 35 kW** is almost certainly cheaper on **AG-A1** (no demand charge, ~$0.69/day) or **AG-A2**. PG&E defaults meters UP to AG-C on a single 35 kW demand spike but never demotes them back automatically — so AG-C meters are the prime 'demote-to-AG-A1' sweep candidates.
- **Demand exposure:** high and structural. This is exactly the rate where a trailing-12-month interval pull most often finds money.
- **Mapping caveat:** this decode is from `brief-pge-ag-rates.md` (Advice 7846-E, eff. 3/1/2026). PG&E re-prices several times a year; re-pull the tariff before billing-grade use. The grower's stored token here is `AGC`.
