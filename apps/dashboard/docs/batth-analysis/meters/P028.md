## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**On the bill: `AG5C` (legacy Schedule AG-5, the small / `<35 kW` tier).**

What AG-5 is, in operator English: AG-5 is one of PG&E's **closed legacy agricultural rates**. PG&E no longer signs anyone up for it; meters on it are grandfathered. It is a time-of-use power rate with a flat per-day **customer (service) charge** plus per-kWh energy split by time-of-use window plus a per-kW **max-demand** charge.

This is by far the heaviest service charge of any plan on the card — for comparison, the current AG-C family is $1.43343/day and AG-B is $0.91565/day. A legacy AG5C meter pays roughly **4x** the daily fixed charge of the current AG-C rate just to keep the meter alive.
- **Max-demand charge:** $14.90/kW (winter and summer). None billed this cycle (zero usage).
- **Peak-period demand (summer only):** $9/kW — representative placeholder, not bill-sourced.
- **TOU energy windows** (the AG RATE peak is **5–8pm year-round daily**, NOT the 4–9pm DR event window): winter peak $0.155, partial-peak $0.125, off-peak $0.11 /kWh; summer peak $0.20, partial $0.15, off-peak $0.125 /kWh (summer energy is a representative placeholder pending a summer bill).

**Why it is on this rate:** It is a grandfathered legacy meter (`legacy: true` in inventory; the AG5C print confirms it). The grower never affirmatively chose AG-5C — it is simply a closed schedule the meter has carried for years. The whole point of the rate-optimization lever is that a meter sitting on a closed AG-4/AG-5 schedule is a prime candidate to be the wrong rate today, because PG&E's current AG-A/AG-B/AG-C rates carry far smaller fixed charges. **For this specific meter the fixed-charge gap alone is large: $5.30871/day vs $1.43343/day (AG-C) or $0.68895/day (AG-A1).**
