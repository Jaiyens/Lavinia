## (b) RATE SCHEDULE, in plain English

- **Stored schedule code (inventory):** `HAGB`
- **Tariff as the latest bill actually prints it:** **"AGB Ag35+ kW Med Use"** —
  i.e. PG&E schedule **AG-B**, the medium-use agricultural time-of-use rate for
  accounts whose maximum demand is **at or above 35 kW**.

What AG-B is, in operator English:
- It is one of PG&E's **current** (not legacy/closed) agricultural rates.
- "Ag35+ kW" = it is the tier intended for pumps that pull **35 kW or more** at
  their peak (a 50 HP motor at full load is roughly 37 kW, so this pump sits
  right at that size break — consistent with being on the 35+ tier).
- "Med Use" = the medium monthly-usage band (AG-B sits between the low-use AG-A
  and high-use AG-C usage tiers).
- It carries a **fixed daily customer (meter) charge**, plus **time-of-use
  energy** priced in three day-parts (Peak / Part-Peak / Off-Peak), plus — for
  the 35+ kW tier — a **demand charge in $/kW** on the monthly peak.

WHY this pump is on this rate: a 50 HP / ~37 kW motor clears PG&E's 35 kW size
break, so it belongs on the **35+ kW** ("B") medium-use agricultural schedule
rather than the small (<35 kW, "A") schedule. That is the structurally correct
home for a pump this size. (Note: this billing cycle is idle, so the demand and
TOU machinery never engaged — see the bill below.)
