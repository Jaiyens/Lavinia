## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**Schedule on file: `AG5B` — i.e. legacy PG&E Agricultural rate AG-5B.**

- **What it is.** AG-5 is one of PG&E's **legacy (closed) agricultural time-of-use rates**. The "B" is a size/tier letter. AG-5 is a *demand-metered* TOU schedule: the customer pays (1) a fixed daily customer charge, (2) a **maximum-demand charge** in $/kW set by the single highest 15-minute power draw in the cycle, and (3) per-kWh energy split across peak / off-peak windows. AG-5 / AG-4 are **closed to new enrollment** — they are a valid *source* rate to move OFF of, never a *target* to move onto (the engine treats the `AG-4`/`AG-5` families as `legacy: true`; see `familyOf()` and `LEGACY_FAMILIES` in `src/lib/energy/rates.ts`).
- **Customer charge.** Printed as a **per-day** rate of **$1.19446/day** (so ≈ $35.83 across this 30-day cycle, prorated 18 days in Feb + 12 days in Mar).
- **Demand $/kW.** The bill's *label text* reads "$21.43000" and "$20.54000" per kW, but those are **vision-extraction artifacts in the label string** — they do not reproduce the printed amounts. The authoritative figures are the **printed/structured demand dollars** ($13.12 and $4.14), which imply an *effective* demand rate of **≈ $12.86/kW** (Feb sub-period) and **≈ $8.21/kW** (Mar sub-period). The engine never trusts the parsed label rate; it reads the dollar back from the data (`demandRate()` in `src/lib/energy/demand.ts` = demand charge ÷ peak kW).
- **TOU windows.** AG-5 prices a **peak** window (PG&E's 5–8pm rate peak in the current code's clock, `src/lib/energy/tou.ts`) and an **off-peak** window (everything else), summer vs winter seasons. *This cycle carried 0 kWh*, so no TOU energy lines printed.
- **WHY it is on this rate.** Legacy inertia: AG-5B is a grandfathered schedule the meter has carried for years; it was never migrated to the current AG-A / AG-B / AG-C family. That is exactly the situation Lever 1 (rate optimization) exists to flag — but see the honesty caveat in section (e): this meter has **no interval kWh and is idle**, so a trustworthy switch-and-save number cannot be produced.
