  prefix; the tariff is **AG-A2**.
- **Schedule as the bill prints it (`latestTariff`):** **"AGA2 Ag<35 kW High Use"**.

**What AG-A2 is.** AG-A2 is PG&E's *current* small-agriculture time-of-use rate
for pumps under **35 kW** of demand but with **higher energy use** than the A1
"Low Use" branch. It is in the same sub-35 kW **AG-A family** as A1. Important
modeling note (`rate-lever.ts` `LABEL_TO_PLAN`): although the *bill* prints
"<35 kW", the rate card **models AG-A2 under its `large` pricing row** while the
schedule's real size eligibility is **`small`**. The engine deliberately tracks
both: it prices on the AG-A2 card row but only ever offers this meter
**small-tier** candidates, so it can never propose a 35-kW+ schedule to a sub-35
pump. AG-A2 is a **current** (non-legacy) schedule, so the legacy-escape finding
does not apply.

**Its price components (dated rate card, effective 2026-03-01):**

- **Customer charge:** **$0.68895 / meter / day** (≈ **$20.96 / month**) — same as
  AG-A1.
- **Demand charge (max demand):** **$21.43 / kW** of monthly billed maximum
  demand, summer and winter alike. (This is the higher AG-A2 demand rate; AG-A1 is
  $12.33/kW.)
- **Energy ($/kWh), time-of-use:**
  - Summer (May-Oct): peak $0.29, partial-peak $0.27, off-peak $0.25 per kWh.
  - Winter (Nov-Apr): peak $0.24571, partial-peak $0.23000, off-peak $0.21642 per kWh.
- **TOU windows:** standard PG&E ag TOU (card stores only `summerMonths
  [5..10]`). Peak ≈ the 4-9 pm evening block; off-peak ≈ overnight/midday. This
  cycle's bill confirms the pattern: most of the kWh is **off-peak** (overnight
  pumping), a smaller slice **peak**.

**Why this meter is on AG-A2.** It is a small (<35 kW) pump — its billed max
demand this cycle was **≈7.4-9.5 kW**, well under the 35 kW break — but it
**actually runs** (1,567.788 kWh this winter cycle), so PG&E's "High Use" small-ag
branch (A2, with the lower per-kWh energy rates traded for a higher demand rate)
