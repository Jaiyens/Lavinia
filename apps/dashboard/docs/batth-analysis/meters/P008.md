## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**On the rate:** **AG-C** — the modern Schedule AG **high-use, 35-kW-and-up** rate. Inventory codes
it `HAGC` and the bill names it **"AGC Ag35+ kW High Use."**

- **`HAGC` decoded:** "HAGC" is **not a separate tariff** — it is PG&E's billing code for **Schedule
  AG rate AG-C taken at transmission (high) voltage** (the "H" prefix = high/transmission voltage).
  Same AG-C rate structure, high-voltage service.
- **Customer charge:** **$1.43343 per meter per day** = about **$43.58/month** (the highest
  customer charge in the modern AG family). On a 365-day year that fixed charge alone is
  **$1.43343 x 365 = $523.20/yr**.
- **Demand $/kW:** AG-C is the demand-heavy rate. It carries a **Max-Demand charge of $21.43/kW
  (secondary voltage)** PLUS a **Summer Peak-Demand charge of $29.92/kW** assessed on the highest
  15-minute demand during the **5-8 pm** window. This meter is idle this cycle, so it pays **$0
  demand**, but the exposure is live whenever it runs.
- **TOU windows (modern Schedule AG):** the defining peak is **5-8 pm** (summer), distinct from the
  legacy noon-6 pm peak on the AG-4/AG-5 meters. Max demand = highest 15-min average in the month;
  max peak-period demand = highest 15-min average during 5-8 pm.

**WHY this meter is on AG-C:** PG&E **defaults a meter to AG-B/AG-C the moment its metered demand
touches 35 kW in any month over the trailing 12 months** — and it does **NOT** automatically demote
it back when the load shrinks. An 800-GPM pump easily exceeds 35 kW when running, so it was placed
on AG-C; but it is **idle this cycle and possibly long-term**, which is exactly the "stranded on the
high-use schedule" trap (see Finding 1). AG-C only "wins" when a meter pushes serious kW to dilute
the big fixed/demand charges; an idle or low-hours pump is the wrong customer for it.

### Finding 1 — Stranded on the AG-C 35+kW high-use schedule -> demote to AG-A1
- **Lens:** `findings-rate-schedule-arbitrage.json` ("Idle/near-zero-demand AGC pumps stranded on
  the 35+ kW high-use schedule (demote to AGA1)"); confidence **medium**, needsIntervalData **true**.
- **Arithmetic (this is the per-meter savings):** AG-C customer charge $1.43343/day; AG-A1 customer
  charge $0.68895/day. Customer-charge saving per meter =
  **($1.43343 - $0.68895) x 365 = $0.74448 x 365 = $271.74/yr**. AG-A1 also carries **NO demand
  charge** (vs AG-C's $21.43/kW max + $29.92/kW summer-peak), so demoting also removes all demand
  exposure for whenever the pump runs at low load. (The lens aggregates this across 10 stranded
  AG-C meters = 10 x $271.74 = $2,717.40/yr; P008 / "PUMP 8" is explicitly one of the 10.)
- **Engine:** the rate-optimization dollar is computed by `rateOptimization`
  (`src/lib/energy/rate-compare.ts:160`) / the canonical sibling `rateLever`
  (`src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`), priced by `cycleCostUnderPlan` / `priceCycleCents`
  (`rates.ts:157,217`) against `fixtures/pge-ag-rate-card.json`, gated by a +/-10% bill-reproduction
  back-test and a >=$200/yr-and->=3% materiality gate. **Deterministic, no LLM** (zero AI imports in
  these files). The $271.74/yr here is the pure customer-charge delta x 365 — itself deterministic
  arithmetic from the two published $/day figures.
- **Have:** the AG-C placement, the $43.00 idle bill, both published customer charges, the
  zero-kWh / zero-demand cycle, status GOOD, 800 GPM.
- **Need (load-bearing caveat):** **trailing-12-month 15-minute interval kWh.** AG-A1 eligibility is
  a TRAILING-12-MONTH ratchet — a single month with metered demand >= 35 kW disqualifies AG-A1 for
  a year. An 800-GPM pump **likely exceeds 35 kW in-season** and may NOT be demotable. The engine's
  rate lever refuses to quote a switch without a passing interval-based recompute; an AG-C -> AG-B/A1
  "savings" emitted without intervals is a sign-ambiguous artifact the gates exist to suppress.
  Must confirm max 15-min demand stayed comfortably <35 kW across all 12 months before electing
  AG-A1. (The lens flags the high-GPM pumps as the riskier demotions; P008 at 800 GPM is mid-pack.)
- **Flag:** $271.74/yr per meter — **below the $500/yr notable threshold on its own** (notable only
  in aggregate across the 10-meter sweep). Contingent on interval data clearing the 35-kW ratchet.

### Note on overlap (do not double-count)
Findings 1 and 2 are **two actions on the same idle dollar**, not additive: either demote the rate
(keep the meter, save ~$271.74/yr on the customer-charge delta) **or** suspend service (save the
full $516.00/yr while idle). The `findings-account-entity-nema-structure.json` lens makes this
explicit — demoting the 6 idle AG-C meters to AG-A1 saves ~$271.56/yr each (~$1,629/yr), while fully
closing the dead services saves the full $43.58 x 12 each. Suspending captures more dollars but
forfeits use; demoting keeps the pump usable. The right move depends on whether P008 will irrigate
again.
