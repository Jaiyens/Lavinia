## (b) RATE SCHEDULE decoded in plain English

- **Inventory rate code:** `AGC` (the bill prints it as **"AGC Ag35+ kW High Use"**). The billing engine's `latestTariff` is **"AGC Ag35+ kW High Use"** across all four cycles.
- **What AG-C is, in operator English:** AG-C is PG&E's *large agricultural* time-of-use schedule for the **35 kW and up** service size ("Ag35+ kW High Use"). It is the demand-charge-carrying ag rate: in addition to a daily customer charge and per-kWh energy by time-of-use, it bills a **monthly maximum-demand charge in $/kW** on the highest 15-minute kW the meter pulls in the cycle, plus (in summer only) a peak-period demand charge.
- **Customer charge:** the bill prints **$1.43343 per day** (the card models AG-C at $1.43343/day = ~$43.60/month). Every P038 cycle's customer charge is `days × $1.43343`.
- **Max-demand $/kW:** the bill printed two different $/kW figures inside the latest straddling cycle because of the **2026-03-01 rate change** — **$26.03000/kW** before 03/01 and **$24.95000/kW** on/after 03/01. The card's reference AG-C max-demand is **$24.95/kW** (post-change), with the published AG-C **Demand Charge Limiter of $0.50/kWh** and a summer peak-period demand adder ($9/kW on AG-C2).
- **TOU windows:** AG RATE peak is **5–8pm year-round daily** (distinct from the 4–9pm DR event window). AG-C winter energy on the card is peak $0.18430, partial-peak $0.17, off-peak $0.15861 per kWh; summer energy is representative ($0.22 / $0.20 / $0.18). **None of these energy prices touched P038's bill** — the meter moved **0 kWh** in every billed cycle (see c/d), so only the customer charge, the tiny max-demand charge, and the NEM net charge appear.
- **WHY this meter is on AG-C:** it is a large (35 kW+) irrigation pump (1,300 GPM, and its solar sibling P031 hit a billed peak of 111.52 kW). Pumps of this size on the Batth master account bill on the current large-ag TOU schedule AG-C. The rate is appropriate for the service size; nothing here suggests a rate-switch.

## (e) EVERY applicable savings finding FOR THIS METER (full arithmetic + engine)

All dollar figures below are produced by **deterministic pure functions in `src/lib/energy`** (no AI). The only AI in the pipeline is the bill-PDF vision extraction in `src/lib/extract`. The orchestrator is `src/lib/recommendations/run.ts::runEngines`.

1. **Rate optimization (`rate-compare.ts::rateOptimization` / `rate-lever.ts::rateLever`, tool `rate-optimization`)** — **NO FINDING.**
   - `runEngines` only runs rate optimization on a meter that is `isAg && intervals.length > 0 && solarKw === null`. **P038 is solar-paired (`solarFlag = true`)**, so it is excluded by design — NEM economics net generation against use, which the gross-consumption model cannot price honestly. Even setting that aside, P038 has **0 kWh** in every cycle, so `rateLever` would return `reason: "no_usage_basis"` (a non-legacy AG-C meter with `totalKwhTested <= 0` fails closed). **Determinism:** pure, no AI. **Data we HAVE:** rate label, four printed cycles, customer/demand line items. **Data we NEED:** 15-minute interval kWh (none on file). Without intervals, any AG-C→AG-B "savings" the engine could emit would be a **sign-ambiguous artifact** (per ground truth) and is suppressed. No finding.

2. **Demand-charge exposure (`retrospective.ts::retrospective`, tool `demand-charge`)** — **NO FINDING.**
   - Gated on `intervals.length > 0 && solarKw === null`. P038 has no intervals and is solar-paired, so the lever never runs. The billed demand here is microscopic anyway ($0.05–$1.98/cycle, peak kW 0.0148–0.076), nowhere near a meaningful demand charge. **Determinism:** pure. **HAVE:** printed demand line items. **NEED:** interval history to attribute a peak to an avoidable day. No finding.

3. **Bill audit (`bill-audit.ts::billAudit`, tool `bill-audit`)** — **NO FINDING.**
   - This is the one engine that runs on every pump with bills (no interval gate). It compares each cycle's total to the median of the meter's **other same-season cycles** and flags a dollars-up-but-usage-flat outlier. P038 has no peak history per cycle, so it would take the **no-peak path** (stricter `noPeakBillTolerance = 0.5`, "watch" severity, needs ≥3 same-season comparators). P038 has only ~4 cycles, all winter-ish and all near $43–46, none exceeding the median by >50%. It also needs `minComparators = 3` same-season peers, which it does not have. **No flag.** **Determinism:** pure median math, no AI. **HAVE:** four printed totals. **NEED:** more same-season cycles for a stable median. No finding.

4. **Solar / NEM checks (`solar-nem.ts::solarNemChecks`, tool `solar`)** — **NO DOLLAR FINDING; only a tracking note on the demo path.**
   - `solarNemChecks` runs only `if (isDemo && pump.solarKw !== null)`. On the **real** Batth farm the canonical `runSolarInsight` (`run-solar-insight.ts` → `nemDemandInsight`) is the sole solar owner (ADR-S05). `nemDemandInsight` requires the meter to be **AG-C family AND reconciled AND owing a demand charge > 0**. P038 *is* AG-C, but its coverage is `needs_review` (not `reconciled`) and its demand owed is essentially $0.36 across cycles — so `nemDemandInsight` returns **null** (fail closed). The legacy `solarNemChecks` (1) demand-peak finding needs `demandChargeUsd > 0 && peakAt in 4–9pm window` — P038 has no `peakAt`, so no demand-peak rec; (2) it would emit a **NEM true-up *tracking* note** (`track_trueup`, severity info, **no dollar**) for the December/March true-up. That note carries no `impactUsd`. **Determinism:** pure. **HAVE:** nemType, trueUpMonth, monthlyNet, annual import/export. **NEED:** the **Generation Allocation Summary** to interpret the export=0 / sibling-anomaly relationship. No standalone dollar finding for P038.

**Net for P038:** **no >$500/yr deterministic finding on this meter itself.** Its analytical value is as the **control/sibling proof** for the P031 anomaly. P031's $62,795.65 true-up is the real anomaly, and its recovery is **$0–$57k CONTINGENT on the Generation Allocation Summary** (the arrays may be oversubscribed = zero-sum); never present P038 or P031 as banked savings.
