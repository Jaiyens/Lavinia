## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**On the rate:** **AG-5B** (PG&E billing code `AG5B`; the bill prints it as "AG5B Large
Time-of-Use Agricultural Power"). AG-5 is a **closed (legacy) Time-of-Use Agricultural** schedule;
the "B" variant is the **large** (35+ kW connected) flavor. In the rate card this maps to the
**AG-5 / large** plan (`LABEL_TO_PLAN["AG5B"] = { family: "AG-5", sizeClass: "large",
realTier: "large" }` in `src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`).

- **Customer charge:** **$1.19446 per meter per day** (AG-5 large). This is the exact per-day rate
  printed on the bill (see section c). Over a 365-day year the fixed customer charge alone is
  **$1.19446 x 365 = $435.98/yr**, paid whether or not the pump runs.
- **Demand $/kW:** AG-5B is a **demand-bearing** rate when the pump actually runs. From the rate
  card the AG-5 large plan carries a **max-demand charge of $20.54/kW (winter)** that applies
  year-round, **plus a summer peak-period demand charge of $10/kW** (the legacy noon-6 pm window,
  card value; representative pending the summer tariff sheet). In this winter credit cycle P002
  billed **zero demand** (no demand line printed). For scale on a sibling AG-5B pump: P004
  (4698660251) billed 171.52 kW = $1,409.21 of demand in the same cycle, so AG-5B demand exposure
  is real and large on a pump that is actively pulling water.
- **TOU windows (legacy AG-5, three-period):** the defining feature is the **legacy noon-6 pm
  summer peak window**, NOT the modern 5-8 pm Schedule-AG peak. Summer Off-Peak covers the late
  evening / overnight / weekend; the legacy partial-peak shoulders surround the noon-6 pm peak.

**WHY this meter is on AG-5B (the important part):** P002 is **NEM2-paired solar** (aggregation
group 4444). The legacy **noon-6 pm peak window overlaps midday solar production**, so the solar
array offsets the most expensive hours of the day. The modern Schedule-AG peak (5-8 pm) no longer
overlaps solar, so moving P002 to a current schedule early would push the expensive window past
sundown and **destroy solar value**. The rate-arbitrage lens
(`findings-rate-schedule-arbitrage.json`) names P002 explicitly as one of the **10 legacy
AG5B/AG5C/AG4C solar-NEM pumps whose rate is intentional, not arbitrage** — hold until the
solar-legacy eligibility lapse (no later than 7/31/2027), at which point AG-5B force-transitions to
AG-C. So the rate is **deliberately held, not a mistake to fix today.**

### Finding 1 — Legacy rate is INTENTIONAL (hold, take NO action before 2027)
- **Lens:** `findings-rate-schedule-arbitrage.json` -> "Legacy AG5B/AG5C/AG4C solar-NEM pumps: rate
  is intentional, not arbitrage (hold until 2027)". P002 (4691688023) is listed by name in the
  meter set.
- **Dollar value: $0.00 (annualUsd 0), confidence HIGH.** This is an explicit **do-not-touch**
  finding. P002 is one of 10 legacy solar-NEM pumps deliberately parked on legacy TOU (noon-6 pm
  peak) to hold the window that overlaps midday solar. Moving it to modern Schedule AG early would
  destroy solar value.
- **Engine / determinism:** the rate-optimization lever (`rateLever`,
  `src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts:443`) would **not quote a switch here** for two independent,
  hard-coded reasons:
  1. **Solar meters are never priced.** The runner (`runRateLever`,
     `src/lib/recommendations/run-rate-lever.ts:94-106`) passes `periods: []` for any meter where
     `isSolar || solarKw !== null`, because a solar meter's monthly charge pages omit the energy
     that settles at true-up; pricing a counterfactual from those pages would mislead. With no
     periods, the lever returns a **qualitative** legacy finding carrying the honest
     `legacySolarNote()` (solar settles at true-up), severity "watch", **impactUsd null** — money
     tracked, never money at stake.
  2. **This is a credit cycle.** Even if it were priced, `cycleFromPeriod`
     (`rate-lever.ts:250`) excludes any period whose `printedTotalCents < 0` with reason
     `credit_cycle` — a consumption-only recompute cannot be back-tested against a net-export
     credit. P002's only ingested cycle is -$149.11, so it is excluded by construction.
- **The AG-C -> AG-B artifact warning:** because AG-5B is a legacy schedule, the engine's *intended*
  behavior is the qualitative hold-note above. Any numeric "savings" the lever might emit on a
  legacy AG meter WITHOUT real 15-minute interval kWh is a **sign-ambiguous artifact** (the back-test
  band in `rate-lever.ts:37` is calibrated against bill summaries, which carry no true interval
  kWh). Do not surface a dollar switch figure for P002.
- **Have:** the AG5B schedule label, the solar/NEM flags, the reconciled (credit) cycle.
- **Need:** nothing to ACT — the action is to **calendar the 2027 force-transition** (AG-5B -> AG-C)
  and re-run the idle/<35 kW AGA1-demotion sweep then, IF the pump is idle at that time. (P002 is
  GOOD and high-GPM, so it is unlikely to be AGA1-demotable; high-GPM pumps breach 35 kW in-season.)
