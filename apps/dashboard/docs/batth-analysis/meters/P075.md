## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**Schedule on file (inventory): `HAGC`.** **Latest tariff printed on the bill: `AGC Ag35+ kW High Use`** — i.e. PG&E **Schedule AG-C**, large-power agricultural, high-use ("Ag 35+ kW") variant. ("HAGC" is the high-use AG-C spelling; the engine's `LABEL_TO_PLAN` map normalizes the `AGC` token to `family: "AG-C", realTier: large`.)

What AG-C is, in operator English:
- **AG-C is a current (not legacy) large agricultural time-of-use rate** for meters 35 kW and up. It is the **demand-metered** large-ag schedule: it normally carries a **monthly maximum-demand charge in $/kW** plus **TOU energy** (peak / off-peak kWh) and a **daily customer charge**.
- **"Ag35+ kW High Use"** is the high-load-factor variant — intended for meters that run a lot of hours (a high use-factor), which trades a higher fixed/customer component for lower energy rates. For a pump that *actually runs*, that is the right shape.
- **Customer charge:** billed **per day at $1.43343/day** (a fixed access charge; here split 18 + 12 days across the 2026-03-01 rate change).
- **Demand charge ($/kW):** AG-C carries a demand charge — but **this cycle billed none** because the meter was idle (no kW set, `peakKw: null`, `demandChargeUsd: null`).
- **TOU windows:** AG-C is time-of-use. The bill prints a **Peak** bucket and an **Off-Peak** bucket — both at **0 kWh / $0.00** this cycle (`touRollup` shows Peak 0.0 / Off-Peak 0.0). The relevant peak for any future demand charge is the evening rate-peak window (`tou.ts`).
- **WHY it is on this rate:** It is a current large-ag assignment (not legacy) for a 1,000-gpm wine-grape pump that, when running, would set a demand and draw real TOU energy. **But right now it is idle**, so the only thing the rate is doing is charging the **daily customer (access) fee**. The interesting question for a high-use rate is whether the meter's *actual* annual load factor justifies "High Use" — answerable only with real usage.
