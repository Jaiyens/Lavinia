## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

- **Stored schedule (inventory):** `HAGA2` → the "H" is the territory/hosting
  prefix; the tariff is **AG-A2**.
- **Schedule as the bill prints it (`latestTariff`):** **"AGA2 Ag<35 kW High Use"**.

**What AG-A2 is.** AG-A2 is PG&E's *current* small-agriculture time-of-use rate
for pumps under **35 kW** of demand but with **higher energy use** than the A1
"Low Use" branch. It sits in the sub-35-kW **AG-A family** alongside AG-A1.
Modeling note (`rate-lever.ts` `LABEL_TO_PLAN`): although the bill prints
"<35 kW", the rate card **models AG-A2 under its `large` pricing row** while the
schedule's published size eligibility is **`small`**. The engine tracks both: it
prices on the AG-A2 card row but only ever offers this meter **small-tier**
candidates (`["AG-A1","AG-A2"]`), so it can never propose a 35-kW+ schedule to a
sub-35 pump. AG-A2 is a **current** (non-legacy) schedule (`isLegacyTou: false`),
so the legacy-escape finding does **not** apply.

**Its price components (dated rate card `fixtures/pge-ag-rate-card.json`,
effective 2026-03-01; values are bill-sourced):**

- **Customer charge:** **$0.68895 / meter / day** (= **$20.96 / month**) — identical
  to AG-A1.
- **Demand charge (max demand):** **$21.43 / kW** of monthly billed maximum
  demand, summer and winter alike. (AG-A1's is the lower **$12.33/kW**.)
- **Energy ($/kWh), time-of-use:**
  - Summer (May-Oct): peak **$0.29**, partial-peak **$0.27**, off-peak **$0.25**.
  - Winter (Nov-Apr): peak **$0.24571**, partial-peak **$0.23000**, off-peak **$0.21642**.
- **TOU windows:** standard PG&E ag TOU (the card stores `summerMonths [5..10]`).
  Peak ≈ the 4-9 pm evening block; off-peak ≈ overnight/midday. This cycle's bill
  confirms the pattern: most kWh is **off-peak** (19.585 of 22.378 kWh), a small
  slice **peak** (2.793 kWh).

**Why this meter is on AG-A2.** It is a small (<35 kW) pump — its billed max
demand this cycle was a **trivial 0.042 kW** (effectively idle), far under the
35-kW break — placed on the "High Use" small-ag branch (A2, which trades higher
demand $/kW for lower per-kWh energy). Whether A2 is the *right* small-ag branch
for a meter this lightly loaded is exactly the open rate question below; the
demand-vs-energy mix that decides A1-vs-A2 cannot be pinned from a summary bill.
