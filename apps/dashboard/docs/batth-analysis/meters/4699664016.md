**What AG-A1 is, plainly:** AG-A1 is PG&E's agricultural rate for **small** services that draw
**under 35 kW** and don't have heavy, steady demand. It has:
- a small daily **customer charge** (here **$0.68895/day**),

  (this meter shows only Peak and Off-Peak; no separate partial-peak line on this cycle), and

  AG-A2; the demand component is small here, ~$12/kW, vs ~$25/kW on the AG-C pump P031).

**Why this meter is on AG-A1:** its peak demand is **1.816 kW** and its monthly energy is
**41.7 kWh** — far below the 35 kW line. A tiny shop load belongs on the small-use AG-A1
schedule; putting it on a demand-heavy rate like AG-C would be wrong. The rate code on file
(AG-A1) and the billed tariff (AG-A1) **agree** — no mismatch here, unlike P031.
