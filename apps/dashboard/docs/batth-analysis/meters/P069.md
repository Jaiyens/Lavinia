## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**Schedule on file (inventory): `HAGA1`.** **Latest tariff printed on the bill:
`AGA1 Ag<35 kW Low Use`** — i.e. PG&E **Schedule AG, sub-rate AG-A1**, the modern (non-legacy),
**under-35-kW, low-use** agricultural rate.

What AG-A1 / HAGA1 is, in operator terms:
- **AG-A1 is the cheapest, simplest AG sub-rate.** It is the **<35 kW, low-load-factor** flavor of
  the modern default Schedule AG. Its defining feature: **NO demand charge at all** — it recovers
  everything through a flat daily customer (access) charge plus per-kWh energy. Because it has no
  demand charge, its **energy rates are the highest** of the four AG sub-rates (peak-summer
  ≈ $0.51620/kWh), the trade for never paying a $/kW demand charge.
- **HAGA1** (the inventory code) is **not a separate published tariff** — the `H` prefix is PG&E's
  billing code for **AG-A1 taken at transmission (high) voltage**. The bill itself prints the plain
  **`AGA1 Ag<35 kW Low Use`** label and the AG-A1 **customer charge of $0.68895/day**, so for
  billing-math purposes it is AG-A1-class. (Voltage only matters for demand-charge columns, and
  AG-A1 has **no demand charge in any voltage class**, so the H/transmission distinction has zero
  dollar effect here.)
- **Customer charge: $0.68895 per meter per day** (≈ $20.95/mo at 30.4 days). This is the **lowest
  customer charge in the modern AG family** (AG-A1 = AG-A2 = $0.68895; AG-B = $0.91565;
  AG-C = $1.43343). It is exactly what this meter prints (section c).
- **Demand $/kW:** **NONE.** AG-A1 carries no Max-Demand and no Peak-Demand charge in any voltage
  class. So there is no $/kW exposure on this meter at all.
- **TOU windows (modern AG):** Summer = Jun 1–Sep 30, Winter = Oct 1–May 31; **Peak 5:00–8:00 p.m.
  every day, Off-Peak all other hours** (no partial-peak on the modern AG rates). This cycle's TOU
  rollup is **Peak 0 kWh @ $0 and Off-Peak 0 kWh @ $0** — the pump drew no energy, so the TOU split
  is moot.

**WHY this meter is on AG-A1 (the important part):** AG-A1 is the **correct, already-optimal**
landing spot for a small (<35 kW), low-hours pump. PG&E places a meter on AG-A1/AG-A2 when its
rated capacity is **under 35 kW** and on the low-use (A1) variant when its load factor is low. A
150-GPM pump is **small** — almost certainly well under the 35-kW cutoff — so AG-A1 is exactly
where a low-flow, intermittently-run pump should sit. **This is the single most important rate fact
about P069: it is ALREADY on the cheapest AG customer charge.** There is no cheaper AG sub-rate to
demote it to. The standard Batth "demote idle AG-C → AG-A1" arbitrage move (which saves ~$272/yr on
the AG-C meters like P075/P077) **does not apply here — P069 is already on AG-A1.** The only
remaining lever is what to do with a *dead pump still paying the AG-A1 daily charge* (section e).

## (e) EVERY APPLICABLE SAVINGS FINDING FOR THIS METER

All dollars below are computed by **deterministic, pure, unit-tested functions in
`src/lib/energy`** (priced against the published rate card `fixtures/pge-ag-rate-card.json` and
Batth's own posted bills). **No AI is involved in any savings number.** The only AI in the pipeline
is bill-PDF vision extraction (`src/lib/extract/reader.ts`), which produced the structured numbers
above and **never computes a savings dollar**; its output is hard-gated by a one-cent reconciliation
check before it is trusted.

P069's situation is unusually simple because of two facts that **kill most of the levers up front**:
(1) it is **already on AG-A1** (cheapest AG customer charge — no rate to demote to), and (2) it has
**no solar** (no NEM lens). What is left is the **dead-pump standby** fact and the question of whether
an even-cheaper *non-AG* rate exists for a permanently-off meter.

### Finding 1 — Dead pump bleeding the AG-A1 customer charge (de-energize / remove) — the primary finding
- **Lens:** `findings-idle-standby-meters.json` ("Dead pumps (BAD/OLD status) still energized").
- **Verdict:** P069 is grower-flagged **BAD** (dead) yet still an energized service point that drew
  **zero kWh, zero demand, zero NEM** this cycle and paid a full daily customer charge. Because it
  is on AG-A1 (the cheapest AG access charge), it is the **lowest-floor** dead meter in the cohort.
- **Arithmetic (annualize the printed bill):** **$20.67/mo × 12 cycles = $248.04/yr** of avoidable
  fixed customer charge if the service were suspended/removed for the year. (Cross-check against the
  365-day rate: $0.68895/day × 365 = **$251.47/yr**; the two agree within the month-length rounding,
  $248.04–$251.47/yr.) The P017 dossier cites exactly this number — *"the clean hard-floor dead-meter
  number in this lens is P069 at $248.04/yr"* — confirming the figure across the analysis.
  **Confidence: medium.**
- **Engine / function:** there is **no dollar emitter** for the idle/standby category in code. The
  relevant deterministic logic is `classifyMeter` / `meterSignature`
  (`src/lib/energy/classify.ts:133,67`), which labels a meter pump-vs-non_pump from its usage shape
  (`loadFactor = avgKw / peakKw`) for **legibility only** — the type contract is explicit that a
  status flag yields **no efficiency number** (`src/lib/recommendations/types.ts:16-18`). So the
  $248.04/yr here is **not a fabricated efficiency estimate**; it is the meter's **own printed bill
  annualized** (`BillingPeriod.totalBillUsd × 12`), read straight from the data — **deterministic,
  not AI**.
- **Honest caveats (do NOT overstate):**
  1. **Below the $500/yr notable threshold.** At $248.04/yr this is real but **modest** — it does
     not clear the >$500/yr flag bar.
  2. **Seasonal-vs-permanent.** The $248/yr assumes the idle cycle is representative of all 12
     months. Even though it is BAD-flagged (suggesting permanently dead, not seasonal), confirm
     with trailing-12-month interval data before treating it as a full-year recoverable.
  3. **PG&E removal is effectively permanent + costed.** Meter removal/temporary disconnect carries
     reconnection fees, a ~12-month AG minimum-term stickiness, and (for removal) new-service /
     line-extension cost to ever bring it back. Only pursue if the pump is genuinely abandoned, not
     awaiting a motor swap. Action = **confirm-dead → request PG&E de-energize/remove**, not a
     blind cut.
  4. **No NEM-orphan risk here** (unlike the BAD solar pumps P017/P018): P069 has no solar, so
     removing it cannot collapse or orphan any NEM aggregation group. This is the *clean* dead-meter
     case — the safest one to actually pull.
- **Data we HAVE:** the BAD status flag, the single reconciled idle cycle (0 kWh, 0 demand), the
  printed $20.67, the AG-A1 per-day customer charge, lat/long, GPM, crop, the fact it has no solar.
- **Data we NEED:** **trailing-12-month 15-minute interval kWh** for this SA (to confirm it is
  year-round dead, not seasonally off) **and** an operator confirmation that P069 is truly abandoned
  (not awaiting repair) before filing a PG&E de-energize/remove request.

### Finding 2 — Rate optimization (switch-and-save) — engine: `rateOptimization` / `rateLever` — DOES NOT FIRE
- **Verdict:** **No dollar emitted, correctly.** Two independent reasons:
  1. **Already on the cheapest AG sub-rate.** AG-A1 has the lowest AG customer charge and no demand
     charge. The eligible-target set excludes same-family/same-row rates; for a small (<35 kW)
     low-use meter there is **no cheaper AG landing spot to switch to**. The usual "demote to AG-A1"
     arbitrage that earns ~$272/yr on the idle AG-C meters is **inapplicable — P069 is the
     destination, not a candidate.**
  2. **Idle / no usage basis.** The single cycle on file is **idle (0 kWh)**. The rate lever
     requires `totalKwhTested > 0` and a passing **±10% bill-reproduction back-test over real
     15-minute intervals** before it will quote any switch (`rate-compare.ts:160,186-214`;
     `run.ts:168` gates on `intervals.length > 0`). With 0 kWh and no intervals, `rateLever`
     returns `kind: "none", reason: "no_usage_basis"` and **no AG-A1→anything "savings" is
     emitted.** Per ground truth, any such number computed without interval kWh would be a
     **sign-ambiguous artifact** anyway — the gates exist precisely to suppress it.
- **Engine / function:** `rateOptimization` (`src/lib/energy/rate-compare.ts:160`) / canonical
  sibling `rateLever` (`src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`), priced by `cycleCostUnderPlan` /
  `priceCycleCents` (`rates.ts:157,217`) against `loadRateCard()`. **Deterministic, no LLM.**
- **The one theoretical non-AG angle (not yet quantifiable):** a *permanently dead* pump that will
  never pump again is arguably mis-classed as an **agricultural** service at all (Schedule AG
  requires ≥70% ag end-use). But the right answer for a dead pump is **removal (Finding 1), not a
  rate switch** — there is no cheaper "do-nothing" rate worth the switching cost, and B-1/B-19
  business rates do not apply to a zero-load point. So this stays a removal question, not a rate
  question.
- **Data NEED:** real 15-minute interval kWh + at least one running cycle would be required to even
  attempt a rate back-test — but for a confirmed-dead pump the decision is removal, so interval data
  is needed for the *removal/seasonality* question (Finding 1), not for a rate switch.
