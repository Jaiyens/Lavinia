## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**On the rate:** inventory codes it **HAGA1**; the bill prints it as **"AGA1 Ag<35 kW Low Use"**.
Both resolve to **Schedule AG-A1** — the modern, **small (under 35 kW) low-use agricultural** TOU
schedule. "HAGA1" is the high-voltage (transmission/primary) flavor of the same AG-A1 rate; on the
rate card it maps to the **AG-A / small** plan (`LABEL_TO_PLAN["AGA1"] = { family: "AG-A",
sizeClass: "small", realTier: "small" }` in `src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`).

- **Customer charge:** **$0.68895 per meter per day** (AG-A1). This is the exact per-day rate
  printed on the bill (see section c). Over a 365-day year the fixed customer charge alone is
  **$0.68895 x 365 = $251.47/yr**. AG-A1 is the **lowest customer charge of the current ag family**
  (cf. AG-B at $0.91565/day, AG-C at $1.43343/day).
- **Demand $/kW:** AG-A1 is, in practice, a **near-zero-demand** schedule for a load this small. The
  rate card lists a max-demand $/kW for AG-A1 (12.33/kW), but on this cycle the meter billed only a
  **$0.17 demand charge on a 0.0228 kW peak** — i.e. effectively nothing, because the connected load
  is tiny. AG-A1 carries **no separate peak-period (summer) demand charge**. This is the key reason
  the meter belongs here and not on AG-C: a low-use shop should never pay the AG-C high-use customer
  charge or its $24.95/kW max-demand exposure.
- **TOU windows (modern Schedule AG, two-period):** **Peak 5-8 pm** and **Off-Peak** the rest of the
  day, year-round. The bill split this cycle's tiny usage into a Peak and an Off-Peak energy line
  (see section c).

**WHY this meter is on AG-A1 (the important part):** "Ag<35 kW Low Use" is exactly correct for a
**small, low-use, non-irrigation shop load**. It is below the 35 kW size break (peak this cycle was
0.0228 kW — four orders of magnitude under the break), so it qualifies for the small tier, and the
"Low Use" variant minimizes the fixed customer charge. This meter is **already on the cheapest
eligible schedule** — there is no cheaper current ag rate to move it to. (Note: this is a different,
already-optimized meter from "FARM SHOP SWANSON" / SA 4699664794, which sits on AG-A2 *with* a
demand charge and IS a rate/demand review candidate; do not conflate them.)
