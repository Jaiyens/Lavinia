## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**Schedule on the latest bill: `AG5C` (AG-5C, "Large TOU Ag Power," high-use tier).** This is
a **closed/legacy** large-agricultural time-of-use schedule (inventory `legacy: true`).

What AG-5C is, in operator terms:
- It is one of PG&E's old large-ag TOU schedules (the AG-5 family: A/D, B/E, C/F sub-rates).
  AG-5C is the **high-use ≥35-HP tier**. Its defining trait is **heavy demand charges with
  very cheap energy** — you pay a lot per kW of peak draw but little per kWh.
- **Customer charge: $5.30871 per meter per day** (the highest customer charge of any
  schedule on this farm — AG-5C's signature fixed cost). On the latest bill that prints as
  159.26 dollars of customer charge over ~30 days (see section c).
- **Demand $/kW (from this meter's own bill):** the latest cycle shows a Max-Demand line at
  **$15.56/kW** (Feb sub-month) and **$14.90/kW** (Mar sub-month), plus a Max Part-Peak
  demand at **$3.67/kW** then **$3.51/kW**. The canonical structured demand charge for the
  cycle is **$3.01** on a billed peak of **0.16 kW** (these rates are read straight off the
  print, never hardcoded).
- **TOU windows (legacy):** AG-5/legacy schedules hold the **noon–6 p.m. peak window**. That
  is the whole point of keeping this meter on the legacy rate (see "why" below). Energy on
  AG-5C is cheap per the brief (AG-5C summer peak ~$0.086/kWh generation component; PG&E adds
  delivery), with the money concentrated in the demand columns.

**WHY this meter is on AG-5C (intentional, not a mistake):** P062 is **NEM2-paired** (solar
group 4433). Batth's solar pumps are deliberately **parked on the legacy AG-4C/AG-5B/AG-5C
schedules precisely to hold the legacy noon–6 p.m. TOU peak window**, which front-loads the
midday solar production curve directly against the peak hours. The modern Schedule AG peak
moved to **5–8 p.m.**, which no longer overlaps midday solar — so moving this meter to a
modern AG rate early would *destroy* solar value, not save money. The legacy solar-grandfather
holds until eligibility lapses (no later than **7/31/2027**), at which point AG-5C
force-transitions to **AG-C**. The rate-arbitrage lens explicitly classifies P062 as
**"rate is intentional, not arbitrage — hold until 2027"** (annualUsd = $0).
