- **What the engine would do:** the stored label `HAGB` / printed `AGB` maps via `mapScheduleLabel()` to the **AG-B (large) card plan** (`AGB → family AG-B, realTier large`). The candidate set for a large meter is **AG-B2 and AG-C2**; since the meter is already on AG-B, the only alternative is **AG-C2**. The lever then runs the **back-test gate**: recompute this cycle's $187.57 from the card and require the recompute to land within **±5% (per-cycle ±10%)** before quoting any savings.
- **Why this is NOT trustworthy here (ground truth):** the meter has **zero interval data** (`intervals: []`) and only **ONE billing cycle**. The single cycle straddles the 03/01 rate change, and the bill summary carries no independent 15-min kWh — so any AG-B→AG-C "savings" the engine emits without intervals is, per ground truth, a **sign-ambiguous artifact, not a real saving.** AG-C also bills a **summer peak-period demand charge** which the canonical shape cannot price without a peak-window kW, so the lever's own guard **skips AG-C as a candidate whenever a summer cycle is present** — and even the winter-only recompute rests on one unverifiable cycle.
- **Arithmetic sanity check (illustrative only, do NOT quote as a saving):** moving 252.062 kWh and a 5.672 kW demand from AG-B winter rates to AG-C2 winter rates (AG-C winter peak $0.18430, off-peak $0.15861, max-demand $24.95/kW, customer $1.43343/day) would *lower energy* (AG-C energy is cheaper) but *raise the demand and customer charge* (AG-C demand $24.95 vs AG-B $13.95; customer $1.43/day vs $0.92/day). The net is **genuinely ambiguous in sign** on a 30-day, 252-kWh, 5.7-kW shop — exactly the case the ground truth warns is an artifact without intervals.
