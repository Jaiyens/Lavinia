## (b) RATE SCHEDULE decoded in plain English

**Stored schedule: HAGA2**, which the bill prints as **"AGA2 Ag<35 kW High Use"** → card plan **AG-A2** (the AG-A family, the *high-use* sub-rate of PG&E's modern Schedule AG, under-35-kW size class). The "H" prefix in "HAGA2" is PG&E's transmission/high-voltage billing code; however the **bill charges the secondary-voltage customer rate** ($0.68895/day — see (c)), so this meter is **effectively billed as standard secondary AG-A2**. The H code in inventory is a labeling artifact.

What AG-A2 is, in operator English:
- It is the **agricultural small-service TOU schedule, high-use flavor** — PG&E's "Ag<35 kW High Use" rate. It sits between AG-A1 and the 35-kW rates: it carries a **max-demand charge** (unlike AG-A1's zero) but a **lower per-kWh energy** than AG-A1, so it suits a meter that **runs more hours / uses more energy** than a bare shop.
- **Customer (meter) charge:** **$0.68895/day** (≈ $20.96/month), the same as AG-A1 — exactly what this bill printed.
- **Demand charge ($/kW):** AG-A2 carries a **maximum-demand charge of $11.79/kW at secondary voltage** (the published primary-voltage row is $13.95/kW). It has **NO separate summer peak-window demand charge** (that is an AG-C feature). **This is the crux of this meter's finding:** the bill actually charged **$86.41 on a 3.948 kW peak = $21.89/kW**, which is **1.86×** the $11.79 secondary row and **1.57×** the $13.95 primary row — well above any published AG-A2 demand rate (see (e) finding 1).
- **Energy ($/kWh), TOU windows:** this winter cycle billed **peak $0.246/kWh** and **off-peak $0.2167/kWh** — materially lower than AG-A1 (peak ~$0.34, off-peak ~$0.31), which is AG-A2's whole point (cheaper energy in exchange for the demand charge). **Peak window is 5–8pm year-round**; ag summer = May–October.
- **Why this meter is on AG-A2:** a 15 HP vines pump runs **many hours** during the irrigation season, accumulating real energy (2,392 kWh even in this winter cycle). AG-A2's cheaper energy beats AG-A1's higher energy once the kWh base is large enough — so AG-A2 is the **correct AG-A sub-rate for a steady, energy-heavy small pump** (confirmed below: this meter would *lose* money on AG-A1). The meter stays in the small (<35 kW) family because its real demand is ~4 kW, far under the 35 kW break.
