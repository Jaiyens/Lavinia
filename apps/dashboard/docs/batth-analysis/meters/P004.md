## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**Schedule on file / latest tariff: `AG5B`** (PG&E **Schedule AG-5B**, printed on the bill as *"AG5B Large Time-of-Use Agricultural Power"*).

What AG-5 is, in operator English:
- AG-5 is one of PG&E's **legacy (closed) agricultural rates**. The inventory row marks this meter `legacy: true`. New connections cannot be put on AG-5 anymore; the current agricultural families are AG-A (small, <35 kW), AG-B and AG-C (large, 35 kW+). The engine's schedule map (`LABEL_TO_PLAN` in `rate-lever.ts`) classifies `AG5B` as `family: "AG-5", sizeClass: large, realTier: large`.
- The **"B" variant** is a **demand-metered** large-power rate: it carries a **monthly maximum-demand charge in $/kW** in addition to (small) energy and a daily customer charge. This is exactly what we see on the bill — the cost on this meter is **dominated by the demand charge**, not by kWh.
- **Customer charge:** billed **per day** at **$1.19446/day** (a fixed access charge; on this cycle it was prorated across the 2026-03-01 rate change as 18 days + 12 days).
- **Demand charge ($/kW):** the bill prints a **per-kW maximum-demand rate** that changed mid-cycle: **$21.43/kW** before 2026-03-01 and **$20.54/kW** after. (The actual demand dollar that bills is not a naive kW×rate product — see section (c); AG-5 demand has seasonal/partial structure, so the printed line amount is authoritative.)
- **TOU windows:** AG-5B is time-of-use for energy, but **this cycle carried zero kWh** (idle winter meter — `idleZeroKwh: true`, `totalKwh: 0`), so no peak / partial-peak / off-peak energy lines appear. The whole bill is **demand + customer charge** with no energy component. The TOU peak that matters for the *demand* charge is the evening (5–8pm rate peak in `tou.ts`), when solar is nearly off.

**WHY it is on this rate:** It is a **grandfathered legacy assignment** — a large, demand-metered deep-well pump that has sat on AG-5B since before the AG-A/B/C restructuring. It was never migrated. That is precisely the situation the rate-optimization lever exists to flag: a large legacy meter is a prime candidate to move to a current large-tier schedule (AG-B2 or AG-C2). See section (e).

## (e) SAVINGS FINDINGS for P004 — full arithmetic, engine, data have/need

All dollars below are produced by **deterministic pure functions in `src/lib/energy`** (no AI). The only AI anywhere in the pipeline is the bill-PDF **vision extraction** in `src/lib/extract` that produced these line items in the first place.

### Finding 1 — Rate optimization (move off legacy AG-5B) — engine: `rateLever()` in `src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`
- **What it would do:** AG5B maps to `family AG-5, realTier large` (legacy, closed). The lever's candidate set for a large meter is `["AG-B2", "AG-C2"]` (current large-tier ag schedules). It would recompute this meter's billed cycles on each candidate and quote the cheapest.
- **Deterministic gate it must pass:** `backTestMeter()` recomputes the *current* AG-5B charges from the dated rate card and the meter's own billed usage, and only quotes a dollar if the recompute lands within **±5% (`BACK_TEST_BAND_PCT`)** of the printed totals, with no single cycle off by more than ±10%.
- **Why it CANNOT quote a trustworthy dollar today:**
  1. **Zero usable cycles.** Only one cycle is on file and it is **idle (0 kWh)**. `cycleFromPeriod` excludes a `zero_total`? No — the total is $3,642.22, positive — but the back-test of a **demand-only, idle winter cycle** is dominated by demand structure the card models poorly, and there is **no energy basis**. For a non-legacy candidate the lever requires `totalKwhTested > 0` (`no_usage_basis`); for the legacy current plan it can only emit a **qualitative** "you are on a closed legacy schedule, worth a review" finding, with **no dollar**.
  2. **No 15-minute interval kWh.** Ground truth: rate-optimization needs interval kWh to be trustworthy; bill summaries carry no kWh. The AG-C peak-period demand candidate is explicitly skipped on summer cycles without a parsed peak-window kW (`peakWindowDemandKw: null`). So any AG-5B→AG-B/AG-C dollar the engine emits from this summary-only meter would be a **sign-ambiguous artifact** and must not be quoted.
- **Expected engine output today:** `kind: "qualitative", isLegacy: true, reason: "no_testable_cycles"` (or `no_usage_basis`) — i.e. **"on a closed legacy rate, review it"** with **no dollar figure**. Honest and fail-closed.
- **Data we HAVE:** schedule label (AG5B), one demand-only cycle, the dated rate card.
- **Data we NEED to put a real dollar on it:** **15-minute interval kWh for a full summer cycle** (and ideally 12 months), so the back-test gate can pass and AG-B2/AG-C2 can be priced against real TOU usage and a real peak-window demand. This is the headline lever for the farm and is plausibly large on a 2,250-gpm pump — but **unquantified until intervals arrive.**

### Finding 2 — Solar does NOT cover the demand charge (F2) — engine: `solarNemChecks()` / `nemDemandInsight()` in `src/lib/energy/solar-nem.ts`
- **The honest signal:** this meter nets to an energy credit, yet pays **$3,606.39 of demand charge** this cycle because the demand peak is set in the evening when solar is off. `solarNemChecks` emits the F2 finding when the biggest demand charge is set in the 4–9pm window (it needs `peakAt`); `nemDemandInsight` is the canonical-shape version gated to AG-C reconciled meters.
- **Arithmetic available now:** demand owed this cycle = **$3,606.39**. The `demandUncoveredShare()` ratio = `demandCents / (demandCents + offsettableCents)`. With **0 offsettable energy this idle cycle**, `offsettableCents` is `null` → the function **fails closed (returns null)** rather than quoting a fake 100%. Over a real summer cycle the demand-uncovered share would be quotable.
- **Annualized scale of the demand floor (operator estimate, not an engine quote):** this single winter cycle already carries **$3,606 of demand** that solar does not touch. A pump that runs through summer will set far higher summer demand peaks, so the **annual demand-charge floor this meter pays despite net-exporting is well into five figures**. This is a **real, >$500/yr structural finding** — but the engine surfaces it as an *insight* (a ratio + the honest billed demand dollar), **never** as a "solar saved you $X" credit.
- **`nemDemandInsight` gate today:** returns **null** for P004 because the gate requires `plan.family === "AG-C"` and this meter is **AG-5**, and requires `coverageState === "reconciled"` (this is `needs_review`). So the canonical insight does **not** fire; only the legacy `solarNemChecks` demand-peak finding can, and only with a `peakAt` we do not yet have.
- **Data we HAVE:** demand charge ($3,606.39), full annual NEM net trace, NEM2AA enrollment, true-up month May.
- **Data we NEED:** the **`peakAt` timestamp** of the demand-setting interval (to confirm it lands in the evening window), interval kWh to compute the offsettable share, and the **Generation Allocation Summary** so we know this aggregation meter's real solar credit share.
