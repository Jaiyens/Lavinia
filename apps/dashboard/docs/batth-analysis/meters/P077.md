## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

**Schedule on the latest bill: `HAGC` (inventory) / printed as `AGC Ag35+ kW High Use` (bill).**

What AGC / HAGC is, in operator terms:
- **AG-C** is the modern Schedule-AG **high-use, ≥35-kW** sub-rate: agricultural, time-of-use,
  **carries a per-kW demand charge** plus the cheapest energy of the AG family. The "Ag35+ kW
  High Use" label on the bill is exactly this.
- **HAGC** (inventory code) is **not a separate tariff** — it is PG&E's billing code for
  **Schedule AG rate AG-C taken at transmission (high) voltage** (the "H" prefix = high/transmission
  voltage). On HAGC the *demand* rows drop to the transmission column (Max Demand $6.45/kW, Summer
  Peak Demand $29.92/kW), but the **customer charge and energy rates are the same AG-C values**.
  The inventory coded this SA HAGC; the **bill prints the AG-C "Ag35+ kW High Use" label and the
  AG-C customer charge of $1.43343/day**, so for billing-math purposes it is AG-C-class. (Note the
  inventory↔bill voltage-code discrepancy: HAGC vs printed AGC. The bill print is authoritative for
  the dollars; the demand columns are moot here because the meter drew zero demand this cycle.)
- **Customer charge: $1.43343 per meter per day** (≈ $43.58/mo at 30.4 days). This is the AG-C
  high-use fixed charge — the highest of the four modern AG sub-rates.
- **Demand $/kW:** AG-C carries a Max-Demand charge ($21.43/kW) and a **$29.92/kW summer 5–8 p.m.
  peak-demand charge**. **None billed this cycle** (peak kW = null, zero demand).
- **TOU windows (modern):** Peak / Off-Peak buckets; modern AG peak is the late **5–8 p.m.** window.
  This cycle's TOU rollup is **Peak 0 kWh @ $0 and Off-Peak 0 kWh @ $0** — the pump drew no energy.

**WHY this meter is on AG-C:** AG-C/AG-B (the ≥35-kW classes) are where PG&E **defaults** a pump
the moment its metered max demand touches **35 kW in any single month over the trailing 12 months**
— a one-way ratchet. A 1,400-GPM almond pump pulls serious kW in-season, so it was (correctly)
sized into the 35+ class. **But AG-C only "wins" when the meter pushes real kW across enough cheap-
energy kWh.** When it sits idle — as it does this cycle (0 kWh, 0 demand) — it pays the **$1.43/day
AG-C high-use customer charge for nothing** and is exposed to demand charges it doesn't need. That
is the basis of the rate-arbitrage and idle-standby findings (section e).

### Finding 2 — Rate arbitrage: demote idle AG-C → AG-A1 (customer-charge delta only)
- **Verdict:** *If* the pump is kept energized (re-irrigating seasonally, so suspension is wrong),
  the next-best move is to **demote it from AG-C to AG-A1** — provided trailing-12-month max

- **Arithmetic (per meter, demote, mutually exclusive with Finding 1):** AG-C customer charge

  demotions in this cohort are the low-gpm meters (PUMP 55 @250 gpm, P072 @300 gpm), **not** P077.
- **Do NOT double-count Finding 1 and Finding 2:** a *suspended* meter saves the **whole** $516/yr;

  alternatives, not additive.
- **Engine / function:** the rate-optimization lever is `rateOptimization`
