## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

- **Stored schedule code (inventory):** `HAGC`
- **Schedule the bill actually prints:** `AGC Ag35+ kW High Use` — i.e. **PG&E Schedule
  AG-C, the large agricultural ("35+ kW / High Use") demand-metered pump rate.** The
  inventory's `H`-prefix (`HAGC`) is the High-use AG-C marker; both resolve to the same
  AG-C family.

**What AG-C is.** AG-C is the **large agricultural pumping rate** for service points whose
maximum demand runs at or above **35 kW**. Unlike the small ag rates (AG-A1/AG-A2), AG-C is
a **demand-metered** schedule. It has three moving parts:

1. A **customer charge** billed per day — here **$1.43343/day** (= **$43.00 over a 30-day
   month**, = **$523.20/yr**). This is the highest fixed daily charge in the ag family.
2. A **demand charge** in $/kW: AG-C carries a **year-round max-demand charge** (~$21.43/kW
   on the card) **plus a summer peak-period demand charge** (~$29.92/kW), with a published
   **Demand Charge Limiter** that caps the summer peak-period component. On this idle cycle
   the demand component is **$0.00** because there was no measured demand.
3. **TOU energy** in $/kWh split into **Peak / Partial-Peak / Off-Peak** windows. On this
   cycle the energy is **$0.00** because there was zero kWh.

**Why this meter is on AG-C.** AG-C is the **default high-use schedule for a large
irrigation pump** that is *expected* to breach 35 kW in season. Batth (or PG&E at
installation) placed P072 on AG-C because, when irrigating, a 300-gpm pump can pull tens of
kW. The catch — and the entire savings story below — is the **trailing-12-month demand
ratchet**: AG-C makes sense only if the pump *actually* runs ≥35 kW at some point in the
year. If P072 stays well under 35 kW all year, AG-C is the **wrong, more-expensive rate**
and the pump belongs on AG-A1. We cannot know which is true without interval data; the bill
summary alone (zero kWh, zero demand) is **sign-ambiguous** about whether this is a
seasonal lull or a genuinely small load.

### Finding 1 — Seasonally-idle pump paying the customer charge with zero use → suspend / move to standby

- **What it is:** P072 is one of **13 GOOD-status, non-NEM pumps** on account
  `4699664587-8` that drew **zero kWh, zero demand, zero NEM** in the latest billed cycle
  yet still paid a fixed customer charge. P072 is one of the **six AG-C members** of that
  set (alongside SIDs 4699142630, P075/4692166716, P077/4699664728, PUMP#8/4692424863, and
  IRR-100HP/4697631144).
- **Arithmetic for P072 specifically:**
  - Printed idle bill = **$43.00/cycle**.
  - Annualized (12 × the printed idle bill) = **$43.00 × 12 = $516.00/yr** if suspended.
  - This is the per-meter figure that rolls into the finding's account-wide total
    (6 AG-C × $516.00 = $3,096.00; plus AG-B and AG-A tiers = **$4,995.48/yr** across all
    13 idle pumps).
- **Engine / function:** this is an **idle/standby lens**, surfaced by the meter coverage +
  retrospective path — `deriveMeterCoverage()` / `deriveAccountCoverage()`
  (`src/lib/energy/reconcile.ts`) flag the zero-energy, non-NEM cycle (`idleZeroKwh`), and
  the annualization is the printed bill × 12. **All deterministic pure functions, NO AI.**
- **CONFIDENCE: medium.** The $516/yr **assumes this idle cycle is representative of all 12
  months.** If P072 is only *seasonally* idle (off in winter, irrigating in summer), the
  realizable standby saving is **roughly half** (~$258/yr), and the pump must stay
  re-energizable.
- **Recommendation:** **SUSPEND, do not remove** — P072 is GOOD and must be reconnectable.
  Pull trailing-12-month interval data first; only a *year-round-idle* P072 justifies a
  PG&E temporary service disconnect / seasonal turn-off (which stops the daily customer
  charge while preserving the service point — no new line-extension cost). CAVEAT: AG
  service has a **12-month minimum-term stickiness** and reconnect fees, so suspension is
  only worth it for long contiguous idle stretches.
- **Data we HAVE:** one reconciled idle winter bill (zero kWh, zero demand, GOOD status,
  non-NEM). **Data we NEED:** trailing-12-month 15-minute interval kWh to confirm P072 is
  idle *year-round* (true standby) vs merely *off-season*.

### Finding 2 — Idle AG-C pump stranded on the 35+ kW high-use schedule → demote to AG-A1

- **What it is:** P072 is one of **10 AG-C meters** on account `4699664587-8` showing
  **near-zero load** this cycle (7 print exactly $43.00 = pure AG-C customer charge). If a
  pump never breaches 35 kW, it does not belong on AG-C at all — it belongs on **AG-A1**,
  which has a **far lower customer charge and NO demand charge**.
- **Arithmetic for P072 specifically (the customer-charge delta):**
  - AG-C customer charge = **$1.43343/day** → **× 365 = $523.20/yr**.
  - AG-A1 customer charge = **$0.68895/day** → **× 365 = $251.47/yr**.
  - Per-meter saving = (**$1.43343 − $0.68895**) **× 365 = $0.74448 × 365 = $271.74/yr**.
  - Across all 10 stranded AG-C meters: 10 × $271.74 = **$2,717.40/yr** (the finding's
    headline total).
  - **Plus**, demoting removes **all AG-C demand exposure** (AG-A1 carries no $/kW max-
    demand and no summer peak-period demand charge), so in any month P072 *did* irrigate
    on AG-C it would also avoid the ~$21.43/kW max-demand + ~$29.92/kW summer-peak charges.
- **Engine / function:** the **rate-optimization lever** — `rateLever()`
  (`src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`), runner `runRateLever()`
  (`src/lib/recommendations/run-rate-lever.ts`), tool key `rate-optimization`.
  `mapScheduleLabel("AGC Ag35+ kW High Use", …)` tokenizes to **AGC** →
  `{ family: "AG-C", sizeClass: "large" }` in `LABEL_TO_PLAN`; the candidate demotion set
  for a large meter is `["AG-B2","AG-C2"]`, and the small-rate counterfactual (AG-A1/AG-A2)
  is priced by `priceCycleCents()` / `cycleCostUnderPlan()`. **All deterministic pure
  functions, NO AI** (the only AI anywhere is the bill-PDF vision extraction in
  `src/lib/extract`, which is upstream of this).
- **HONESTY CAVEAT (critical):** with **zero kWh this cycle**, the engine's energy-side
  AG-C→AG-A comparison is **sign-ambiguous** — there is no usage to price, so any energy
  "savings" the lever emits without interval data is an **artifact, not a banked dollar.**
  The **only trustworthy number here is the fixed customer-charge delta ($271.74/yr)**,
  because that is independent of usage. AG-A1 eligibility is a **trailing-12-month ratchet**:
  a *single* in-season month ≥35 kW disqualifies AG-A1 for a year. A 300-gpm pump *can*
  exceed 35 kW when running, so P072 is a **plausible but unproven** demotion.
- **Recommendation:** P072 is explicitly called out as one of the **safest demotions to
  start with** (low-gpm: "PUMP 55 @250 gpm, P072 @300 gpm"). Pull trailing-12-month
  interval data; if max 15-min demand stayed comfortably <35 kW across all 12 months,
  re-elect **AG-A1** and capture **~$271.74/yr**. Do **not** demote if P072 is about to
  ramp up seasonally (12-month stickiness).
- **Data we HAVE:** the AG-C customer charge ($1.43343/day) and the printed idle bill.
  **Data we NEED:** trailing-12-month 15-minute interval kWh proving P072 never breaches
  35 kW — without it, only the $271.74/yr customer-charge delta is defensible, and the
  energy-side comparison must stay suppressed.
