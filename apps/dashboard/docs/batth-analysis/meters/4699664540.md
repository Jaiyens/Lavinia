  Small Commercial / "Business Low Use."**

**What B-1 is.** B-1 is a **non-agricultural small commercial** TOU rate (PG&E's general
small-business schedule), **not** an ag pump rate. It has:

1. A **customer charge** billed per day — here **$0.82136/day**.
2. **TOU energy** in $/kWh split three ways: **Peak**, **Off-Peak**, and **Super
   Off-Peak**. On this bill those ran **$0.39899 / $0.38287 / $0.36291 per kWh**
   respectively — *much* higher per-kWh than the ag AG-C pump rate next door on P054
   (~$0.16/kWh off-peak), because small commercial energy is simply priced higher.
3. **No demand charge** on this low-use tier (demand line = $0.00).

**Why this meter is on B-1.** It is a **small, non-irrigation load** — the bill marks it
"Bus Low Use" and it pulled only **2,583.75 kWh** with **no demand charge at all**. It is
correctly classified as small commercial, so PG&E bills it on a general business rate, not
an ag schedule. The grower would never put a real pump here; this is a facility/utility
service.

**Key consequence for our engine:** because B-1 is **non-agricultural**, our
rate-optimization lever deliberately **does not touch it** (see section (e)). Per the
verified calibration note, the four non-ag **B1** SAs on this account are *excluded from
the back-test set* precisely because no ag-rate counterfactual is valid for them.

### Finding 1 — Rate optimization → **engine returns NO finding by design (non-ag)**

- **Engine:** `rateLever()` (`src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`), runner `runRateLever()`
  (`src/lib/recommendations/run-rate-lever.ts`), tool key `rate-optimization`.
  Deterministic pure function, **no AI.**
- **What happens on this meter:** `mapScheduleLabel("B1 Bus Low Use", card, …)` tokenizes
  to `B1`, which is **not in the lever's `LABEL_TO_PLAN` table** (that table only knows
  ag families AG-A/AG-B/AG-C/AG-4/AG-5). The map returns **null** ⇒ the lever returns
  `{ kind: "none", reason: "unmapped_schedule" }`. **No recommendation is created, no
  dollar is quoted.** This is correct: you cannot price a small-commercial load against an
  agricultural counterfactual. The runner's comment block and the calibration note both
  call out the **non-ag B1 SAs** as deliberately excluded.
- **The findings rail filter** (`toFindingViews()` in `src/lib/dashboard/findings.ts`)
  also drops any row with no dollar and no impact note (the AC5 honesty law), so even a
  stray informational row would not surface as a savings claim.
- **Data we HAVE:** one reconciled winter bill. **Data we NEED to ever quote anything
  here:** confirmation of what the load actually is, and whether a *different
  small-commercial* schedule (e.g. another B-tier or a TOU vs non-TOU business option)
  fits better — which is **out of scope of the current ag-only rate card** and would need
  a commercial rate card the product does not yet carry.
