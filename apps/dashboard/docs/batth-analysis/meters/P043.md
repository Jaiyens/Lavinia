## (b) RATE SCHEDULE decoded in plain English — AG-A1 / HAGA1 (modern Schedule AG, < 35 kW low-use)

**What it is.** AG-A1 is the **modern default** agricultural sub-rate for installations **under 35 kW** with **low use** (few operating hours / low load factor). The inventory code **HAGA1** is simply AG-A1 billed at **transmission (high) voltage** — not a separate tariff; use the AG-A1 column of Schedule AG. The bill's own label confirms it: "AGA1 Ag <35 kW Low Use."

**Its charge structure (total bundled, eff. 3/1/2026, from the rate brief):**
- **Customer charge: $0.68895 / meter / day** (≈ $20.95/mo at 30.4 days). This is the line on P043's bill.
- **Demand charge: NONE.** AG-A1 has **no demand charge at all** — no max-demand, no peak-demand. It recovers everything through a tiny daily charge + per-kWh energy. This is the key feature: a low-use pump on AG-A1 is **never exposed to a demand-charge spike.**
- **Energy (the trade-off):** AG-A1's per-kWh energy is the *highest* of the four AG sub-rates (peak-summer **$0.51620/kWh**, off-peak summer $0.35026, peak winter $0.33989, off-peak winter $0.31060) — because it recovers everything through energy. That is fine for a meter that moves few kWh.
- **Season:** Summer = Jun 1–Sep 30, Winter = Oct 1–May 31. **Peak: 5–8 p.m. every day**, Off-Peak all other hours. No partial-peak on modern AG rates.

**WHY this meter is on AG-A1 (and why that is right).** P043 is a **< 35 kW, low-utilization** pump that is **not solar-paired**. With no demand charge and a tiny daily charge, AG-A1 is the cheapest rate for a pump that barely runs — exactly the rate the rate brief recommends a low-load-factor meter sit on ("If the meter has low annual operating hours / low total kWh → AG-A1; no demand charge, you eat the higher energy rate on few kWh"). P043 is **already on the optimal rate** for its load profile. The arbitrage opportunity in the fleet is the *opposite* direction — stranded AG-C meters that should be demoted *to* AG-A1; P043 is already there.
