## (b) RATE SCHEDULE — decoded in plain English

- **Stored schedule code (inventory):** `HAGC`
- **Schedule the bill actually prints:** `AGC Ag35+ kW High Use` — i.e. **PG&E
  Schedule AG-C, large tier (the engine maps this to the card row AG-C2)**.

**What AG-C is.** AG-C is PG&E's *demand-metered* current agricultural rate for pumps
that pull **more than 35 kW**. It has three pieces every month:

1. A **customer charge** billed per day. On this bill it printed at **$1.43343/day**
   (matches the card's AG-C row exactly).
2. A **max-demand charge** in $/kW, applied to the single highest 15-minute demand the
   meter hit in the cycle. The reference card models AG-C2 winter max-demand at
   **$24.95/kW**; the bill itself printed a winter demand sub-period at **$26.03/kW**
   (riders push the printed rate a little above the base card $/kW).
3. **TOU energy** in $/kWh, split Peak (5–8 pm rate window, year-round) vs Off-Peak.
   On this winter bill, energy ran roughly **$0.184–0.186/kWh Peak** and
   **$0.159–0.160/kWh Off-Peak**.
4. AG-C also carries a **summer peak-period demand charge** (card models **$9/kW** for
   the large tier) and a **Demand Charge Limiter** — but those only bite May–Oct.
   This bill is a winter cycle, so no summer peak-period demand applies.

**Why this meter is on AG-C.** It draws **278.88 kW peak**, far over the 35 kW
small/large break, so it sits in the large (35+ kW) demand-metered family. AG-C is the
standard current home for a high-use 100 HP pump like this. (Whether AG-C is the
*cheapest* current home is the open question in section (e) — and the honest answer here
is "we cannot prove a switch from one winter bill.")

## (e) SAVINGS FINDINGS — full arithmetic, engine, and data we have vs need

### Finding 1 — Rate optimization (the headline lever) → **NOT a defensible dollar yet**

- **Engine:** `rateLever()` in `src/lib/energy/rate-lever.ts`, driven by the DB runner
  `runRateLever()` in `src/lib/recommendations/run-rate-lever.ts`. Tool key
  `rate-optimization`. **This is a pure, deterministic function** — no AI. It recomputes
  the meter's *own* bill from PG&E's published rate card
  (`fixtures/pge-ag-rate-card.json`) and only quotes savings when that recompute matches
  the printed total inside a calibrated band.
- **How it would run on P054:**
  1. Map `AGC Ag35+ kW High Use` → card plan **AG-C2** (large, current, agricultural).
  2. Extract billed max demand = **278.88 kW** (max of the demand-row 278.88 kW vs the
     text-label 244.32 kW). 278.88 > 35 kW break ⇒ stays in the **large** tier.
  3. **Back-test gate:** recompute this one winter cycle on the AG-C2 card and compare to
     the printed $11,727.33. The engine prices winter max-demand at $24.95/kW once on
     278.88 kW = **$6,958.06**, against the bill's actual two-sub-period demand of
     **$6,599.01** — already ~$359 hot on the demand block alone, before energy/tax
     drift. The published calibration note records this exact pump as the "headline
     $11,727.33 meter" landing at **4.59% aggregate drift**, just inside the 5% band but
     near the per-cycle limit.
  4. **Candidate set:** for a *large* AG-C meter the only current candidate is **AG-B2**
     (AG-C2 is excluded as the current plan; the AG-C summer-peak-demand candidate is
     skipped on summer cycles, not winter). So any "savings" the engine emits here is an
     **AG-C → AG-B move**.
- **Why we must NOT quote this as banked savings:** there is **exactly one cycle, and it
  is winter**, with **no 15-minute interval kWh**. AG-C vs AG-B differ mostly in how they
  charge demand across the *summer* peak window — which this winter bill cannot see. Per
  the verified ground truth, *the AG-C→AG-B "savings" the engine emits without intervals
  are sign-ambiguous artifacts*: the sign can flip once a summer cycle and interval data
  land. The engine's own honesty floor (savings must exceed the model's observed absolute
  error on the meter) is the right guard, and on a single high-drift winter cycle this
  finding should fall back to qualitative / silent rather than print a number.
- **Data we HAVE:** one fully reconciled winter bill, billed peak kW, TOU kWh split,
  AG-C2 card. **Data we NEED to make a trustworthy dollar:** (1) **15-minute interval
  kWh** (to price the AG-B vs AG-C demand windows honestly), and (2) at least one
  **summer** cycle (where AG-C's peak-period demand + limiter actually bite). Until then:
  **no quoted rate-switch dollar for P054.**
